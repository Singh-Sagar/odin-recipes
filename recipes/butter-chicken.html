Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani) is a classic North Indian dish featuring marinated chicken cooked in a creamy tomato-based sauce with spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani) is a beloved dish that originated in Delhi, India, and has since become a staple in Indian restaurants worldwide. This creamy, flavorful curry features tender pieces of chicken that are first marinated in a blend of spices, then grilled or roasted to perfection. The chicken is then simmered in a rich, velvety sauce made with tomatoes, butter, cream, and a carefully curated mix of aromatic spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander.

The sauce is the star of the dish, with its vibrant red color and mild heat coming from the use of Kashmiri chili powder. The combination of tomatoes, butter, and cream creates a luxurious texture that coats each piece of chicken, infusing it with the complex flavors of the spices. Butter Chicken is a relatively mild curry compared to many other Indian dishes, making it a popular choice for those who prefer a more subtle heat. It's typically served with basmati rice or naan bread, allowing diners to soak up every last drop of the delectable sauce

Ingredients

  • Chicken (thighs or breasts), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Yogurt (for marinating)
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Garam masala
  • Turmeric
  • Cumin
  • Chili powder or cayenne pepper
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Heavy cream or evaporated milk
  • Butter or ghee
  • Sugar
  • Fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom
  • Mace
  • Lemon juice
  • Honey or sugar
  • Green chili (optional)
  • Basmati rice or naan bread, for serving

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces, yogurt, garlic, ginger, garam masala, turmeric, cumin, chili powder, and salt. Mix well, cover, and marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add more oil or ghee if needed. Saut√© the onion until translucent, about 3-5 minutes.
  4. Add the tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, heavy cream or evaporated milk, butter or ghee, sugar, fenugreek leaves, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, and mace. Stir well and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  6. Add the cooked chicken pieces back to the pan and stir to coat them with the sauce. Simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened further.
