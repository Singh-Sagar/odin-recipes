Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken tikka masala made easy with this great-tasting recipe — simply marinate chicken breast in yogurt and spices and then simmer in a tomato cream sauce. Serve with rice or warm pita bread.

Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular dish that combines the flavors of tandoori-style chicken with a creamy, tomato-based sauce. The dish originated in the United Kingdom, likely created by a Bangladeshi chef to cater to British tastes, but it has since become a staple in Indian restaurants worldwide.

The dish begins with chunks of chicken that are first marinated in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and herbs, then skewered and cooked in a tandoor oven until charred and tender. These chicken tikka pieces are then added to a rich, creamy sauce made with tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The sauce gets its signature orange-red color and mild heat from the addition of chili powder and paprika. Chicken Tikka Masala is typically served with basmati rice or naan bread, making it a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • Yogurt
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Garam masala
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Chili powder or cayenne pepper
  • Paprika
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Heavy cream or half-and-half
  • Butter or ghee
  • Sugar
  • Fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom
  • Bay leaf
  • Lemon juice
  • Cilantro for garnish
  • Basmati rice or naan bread, for serving

Recipe

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken chunks, yogurt, garlic, ginger, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon cumin, 1/2 teaspoon coriander, 1/4 teaspoon chili powder, 1/4 teaspoon paprika, and salt to taste. Mix well, cover, and marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight in the refrigerator.

  2. Preheat grill or broiler to high heat. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers. Grill or broil the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side, or until cooked through and lightly charred. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-5 minutes.

  4. Add the remaining spices (1/2 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon cumin, 1/2 teaspoon coriander, 1/4 teaspoon chili powder, 1/4 teaspoon paprika), cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaf. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.

  5. Pour in the tomato sauce and water. Add the grilled chicken tikka pieces. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to maintain a simmer. Cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  6. Remove the cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaf. Stir in the heavy cream or half-and-half, butter or ghee, sugar, and fenugreek leaves. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and lemon juice as needed.

  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve over basmati rice or with naan bread.
