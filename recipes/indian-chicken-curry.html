Indian Chicken Curry

This Indian-inspired creamy chicken curry recipe is similar to a curry I had in India. The aromatic spices and flavors are a delight to the senses! Delicious with fresh naan and basmati rice.

Indian Chicken Curry is a beloved dish that features tender pieces of chicken simmered in a rich, flavorful sauce. The chicken is first marinated in a blend of spices, yogurt, garlic, and ginger, which helps infuse it with flavor and keep it moist during cooking. It is then simmered in a sauce made with a base of onions, tomatoes, and a carefully curated mix of aromatic spices like garam masala, cumin, coriander, and chili powder.

The sauce gets its vibrant red color and mild heat from Kashmiri chili powder, while the addition of butter or ghee, cream, and fenugreek leaves creates a luxurious, velvety texture. Indian Chicken Curry is typically served with basmati rice or naan bread, allowing diners to soak up every last drop of the delectable sauce. It is a relatively mild curry compared to many other Indian dishes, making it a popular choice for those who prefer a more subtle heat.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (thighs or breasts), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Yogurt (for marinating)
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Garam masala
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Chili powder or cayenne pepper
  • Turmeric
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Water or chicken broth
  • Heavy cream or evaporated milk (optional)
  • Butter or ghee (optional)
  • Fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom
  • Bay leaf
  • Lemon juice
  • Cilantro for garnish
  • Basmati rice or naan bread, for serving

Recipe

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, yogurt, garlic, ginger, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon cumin, 1/2 teaspoon coriander, 1/4 teaspoon chili powder (if using), 1/4 teaspoon turmeric, and salt to taste. Mix well, cover, and marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight in the refrigerator.

  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add more oil or ghee if needed. Saut√© the onion until translucent, about 3-5 minutes.

  4. Add the remaining spices (1/2 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon cumin, 1/2 teaspoon coriander, 1/4 teaspoon chili powder, 1/4 teaspoon turmeric), cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaf. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.

  5. Pour in the tomato sauce and water or chicken broth. Add the browned chicken and any accumulated juices. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to maintain a simmer. Cook until the sauce thickens slightly and the chicken is cooked through, about 15-20 minutes.

  6. Remove the cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaf. Stir in the heavy cream or evaporated milk (if using), butter or ghee (if using), and lemon juice. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt as needed.

  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve over basmati rice or with naan bread.
