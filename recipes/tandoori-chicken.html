Tandoori Chicken

Try this authentic tandoori chicken that's marinated in yogurt and spices, then cooked on the grill instead of a clay oven so you can make it at home. Serve with long-grain basmati rice, grilled veggies, roasted corn on the cob, and a cucumber salad.

Tandoori Chicken is a beloved Indian dish featuring chicken marinated in a flavorful yogurt-based sauce and cooked in a tandoor, a traditional cylindrical clay oven. The chicken is first skinned and then marinated in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and seasonings like cayenne pepper, red chili powder, turmeric, and tandoori masala. The marinated chicken is then skewered and cooked at high temperatures in the tandoor, which gives it a smoky flavor and charred spots.

Tandoori Chicken can be served as an appetizer or a main course, often accompanied by naan flatbread. It is also used as the base for numerous cream-based curries like butter chicken. The dish became popular worldwide after being introduced by the Moti Mahal restaurant in New Delhi, India in the late 1940s. Today, tandoori chicken is a staple in Indian restaurants around the globe, with many variations like chicken tikka and chicken tikka masala also becoming popular.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (thighs or breasts), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Yogurt (for marinating)
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Garam masala
  • Turmeric
  • Cumin
  • Chili powder or cayenne pepper
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Heavy cream or evaporated milk
  • Butter or ghee
  • Sugar
  • Fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom
  • Mace
  • Lemon juice
  • Honey or sugar
  • Green chili (optional)
  • Basmati rice or naan bread, for serving

Recipe

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, yogurt, garlic, ginger, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric, 3/4 teaspoon cumin, and a pinch of cayenne pepper (if using). Season with salt and pepper, and toss to coat the chicken evenly. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight in the refrigerator.

  2. Melt 4 tablespoons of butter in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 7-8 minutes. Transfer the chicken to a plate, it may not be fully cooked through at this stage.

  3. In the same pot, add the onion mixture and cook, stirring frequently, until beginning to soften, about 2 minutes. Add the remaining spices (1 teaspoon garam masala, 1/4 teaspoon turmeric, 1/2 teaspoon cumin), cinnamon stick, and tomato paste. Cook for 1 minute.

  4. Pour in the tomato sauce and water, then add the browned chicken and any accumulated juices. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to maintain a simmer. Cook until the sauce thickens slightly and the chicken is cooked through, about 8-10 minutes.

  5. Remove the cinnamon stick. Stir in the heavy cream, remaining 2 tablespoons of butter, and lemon juice. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt as needed.

  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve over basmati rice or with naan bread.
